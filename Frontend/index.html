Description

This is a simulation of natural selection which features sheep, wolves, and grass. Grass grows randomly and stays still. Sheep can move and eat grass. Wolves can move and eat sheep. When a sheep or grass has been eaten too much it is removed. Sheep and wolves can detect objects in the simulation to try to follow or avoid them. Sheep and wolves also can have children, which will inherit the parent's neural network. Over time, the sheep should learn to survive over time, or starve/get eaten.

Using the Application

The first button on the left is the “restart” button. Using this button will delete the current simulation from the server, and start a new one. The “data” button provides a little readout, which shows the numbers of each object in the simulation at that instant. It also shows the total energy in the simulation, which is the sum of every object’s energy. The “help” button scrolls the page down to view some information on the simulation, and there is a “back” button to go back up. Lastly, the “configure” button sends the server the current configuration. All the text boxes on the sides of the screen are to change the configuration. None of the changes take effect until the “configure button” has been pressed.
CONFIGURATION:
GRASS:
Energy -> How much energy an object of grass has when created
Size -> How big the grass is
Energy Loss -> How much energy the grass loses when bitten by a sheep
Energy Give -> How much energy a sheep gets from biting the grass
Maximum Amount -> The total amount of grass allowed in the simulation
WOLVES
Speed -> How fast the wolves can move
Size -> How big the wolves are
Mutation Chance -> The chance (0 to 1) that a gene is not inherited but random
Mating Barrier -> How much energy a wolf needs to have to mate
Mating Loss -> How much energy a wolf loses from mating
Mating Cooldown (Parent) -> How long after mating a wolf needs to wait to mate again
Mating Cooldown (Child) -> How long after being born a wolf needs to wait to mate
Child Energy -> How much energy a newborn wolf has
Maximum Amount -> the total amount of wolves allowed in the simulation
View Distance -> How far a wolf can see (detect other objects)
SHEEP
Speed -> How fast the sheep can move
Size -> How big the sheep are
Mutation Chance -> The chance (0 to 1) that a gene is not inherited but random
Mating Barrier -> How much energy a sheep needs to have to mate
Mating Loss -> How much energy a sheep loses from mating
Mating Cooldown (Parent) -> How long after mating a sheep needs to wait to mate again
Mating Cooldown (Child) -> How long after being born a sheep needs to wait to mate
Child Energy -> How much energy a newborn sheep has
Energy Loss -> How much energy the sheep loses when bitten by a wolf
Energy Give -> How much energy a wolf gets from biting the sheep
Maximum Amount -> the total amount of sheep allowed in the simulation
View Distance -> How far a sheep can see (detect other objects)
